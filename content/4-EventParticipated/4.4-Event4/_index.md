# HARVEST REPORT: "FCAJ Community Day 4 - 7 - 2026"

### Event Purpose
* Update on digital transformation trends and career opportunities in the cloud industry in Vietnam.
* Outline the recruitment market paradox and direct essential skills for students and juniors.
* Share hands-on experiences from a Data Engineer regarding business mindset and the differences between university and corporate environments.
* Inspire overcoming fear and building networking skills to discover job opportunities.
* Provide practical advice to help students develop superior thinking and portfolios.

### Speakers List
* **Nguyen Gia Hung** - Head of Solutions Architect AWS
* **Vinh Banh** - Renova Cloud
* **Nhu Tran** - Account Manager AWS
* **Khang Nguyen** - Cloud Kinetics

---

### Event Highlights

#### 1. Cloud Industry Trends & Opportunities (Nguyen Gia Hung)
Affirmed that comprehensive digital transformation is an irreversible trend — core systems such as Core Banking, Finance, and Insurance are being actively migrated to the Cloud. Vietnam is becoming a "Talent Hub": major organizations like NAB (National Australia Bank) are establishing talent centers in Vietnam, with IT workforce sizes reaching 30% of their total global IT staff — surpassing India.

Regarding the market paradox and skill requirements: despite high recruitment demand, opportunities for juniors are limited because enterprises prioritize seniors, forcing students to proactively grow faster to keep up. Regarding AI usage: encouraged students to use AI to solve difficult problems, build strategic thinking, and optimize workflows, rather than using it merely to cheat on assignments.

#### 2. Practical Experience from a Data Engineer (Vinh Banh)
Emphasized the business mindset: IT engineers must not only excel technically but also understand the business culture and customer workflow to deliver the most optimal and valuable technical solutions. Pointed out the difference between university and corporate environments: at university, mistakes only cost points; in enterprise, mistakes carry real responsibility and financial costs — students need to practice discipline and take 100% ownership of their projects.

#### 3. Overcoming Fear & Networking (Nhu Tran)
Discussed common fears: fear of judgment, fear of making mistakes, and pressure from family expectations. The key to overcoming them is practicing honest communication with both managers and colleagues — building a close, trusting relationship. Regarding networking: this is the way to discover opportunities that do not appear on typical job boards (Red Ocean) — every connection and personal project acts as a "seed" for future careers, rather than passively "job hunting."

#### 4. Golden Advice for Students (Khang Nguyen)
Three primary pieces of advice:
* **Always ask "Why":** Inquiry to the very end of a problem to understand the essence of knowledge rather than learning by rote.
* **Embrace Mistakes:** Accept that mistakes are an inevitable part of growth; the key is extracting lessons from each failure.
* **Stay Hungry:** Always exceed expectations. For example, if a project has 10 modules, study and build modules 11 and 12 on your own to enrich your portfolio and practical skills.

---

### Key Takeaways

#### Industry Trends & Career Opportunities
* Migrating to the Cloud is an inevitable digital transformation trend, particularly in core sectors like finance, banking, and insurance.
* Vietnam is becoming a strategic destination for high-quality IT talent for global corporations — presenting a massive opportunity but demanding higher competitiveness.

#### Professional Mindset
* Technical engineers must understand business logic and customer workflows.
* The boundary between university and corporate environments lies in accountability — mistakes in real-world scenarios have actual costs and must be treated with serious discipline.
* AI should be utilized as a tool for strategic thinking and advanced problem-solving, not as a shortcut for assignments.

#### Personal Development
* Overcome the fear of judgment or failure by practicing sincere communication and building real connections.
* Proactive networking is the key to creating career opportunities outside traditional channels.
* Always ask "why," embrace mistakes as growth opportunities, and exceed requirements to build a competitive advantage.

---

### Lessons Learned
* The cloud industry in Vietnam offers massive opportunities but puts high pressure on juniors to mature quickly.
* Technical skills must pair with business thinking and accountability to generate real value for enterprises.
* Mistakes are not something to avoid but an essential part of development — what matters is the lessons learned.
* Career opportunities come from active networking and dedicated personal projects, not just applying for listed jobs.
* "Stay Hungry" — exceeding expectations is the fastest way to stand out in portfolios.
