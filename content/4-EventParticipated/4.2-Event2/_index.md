# Event Report "FCAJ Community Day 30 - 5 - 2026"

## Purpose of the Event
* Introduce tools that help beginners practice AWS safely without worrying about unexpected costs
* Share hands-on experiences from participating in a hackathon
* Inspire and provide skills to build confidence in work and life
* Showcase a real-world AI-powered startup product
* Share lessons on managing hidden risks in project operations and internal personal struggles (procrastination)

## Speakers List
* Huynh Thai Linh
* Huynh An Khuong, Mai Quoc Anh, Nguyen Tran Minh Quan (Team The Ballers)
* Nguyen Thi Quynh Nhu
* Nghia Tran

* Khac Uy Pham

## Key Highlights

**1. Level Up Your AWS Skills with Cloud Quest and Floci (Huynh Thai Linh)**
Pointed out the common fear of AWS beginners: constantly checking the bill in fear of being charged, forgetting to delete resources after practice, and worrying about unexpected costs. Introduced 2 tools to solve this: AWS Cloud Quest — a free, safe playground to practice AWS skills without spending money; and Floci — a tool similar to LocalStack, allowing developers to mock AWS services locally for learning and testing without touching a real AWS account.

**2. Hackathon — The Ballers (Huynh An Khuong, Mai Quoc Anh, Nguyen Tran Minh Quan)**
Introduced the hackathon and why the team decided to participate. Shared the day-by-day journey during the hackathon: challenges faced, how the team found solutions, the emotional rollercoaster, and the opportunities that opened up after completing the competition.

**3. Why We Always Need Confidence (Nguyen Thi Quynh Nhu)**
Discussed confidence: the cost of self-doubt, the science behind psychological struggles, and the power of confidence in changing outcomes. Shared a daily tip to build confidence: when thinking about a task, count down from 5 and take immediate action instead of procrastinating and waiting for the "ready" feeling.

**4. Tu Vi Dai Viet (Nghia Tran)**
Shared the journey of building a horoscope startup website, powered by AI and content-verified by human experts. Presented the problem the product solves and the opportunity in the market.

**5. The Hidden Iceberg of a Project: DevOps Before Disaster (Tran Minh Quan)**
Analyzed the hidden costs of a seemingly simple feature — endless misunderstandings regarding AI that exhausted the project. Used the "iceberg" metaphor: the tip of the iceberg represents visible consequences (missed deadlines, bugs, failures, complaints, burnout); the hidden part underneath represents hard-to-detect root causes such as lack of community spirit in the team and lack of proactive ownership.

**6. The Iceberg of Procrastination (Khac Uy Pham)**
Also used the iceberg metaphor to analyze procrastination: laziness only accounts for 20–30% of the causes, while the vast majority (70–80%) are hidden fears — fear of not being good enough, fear of judgment, fear of failure. Underlying data: 70–80% of procrastination relates to emotional regulation issues, and up to 80% of adults are affected by it. The main message: do not let the iceberg of procrastination freeze your future.

## What We Learned

**Technical**
* There are free and safe tools (Cloud Quest, Floci) to practice AWS without worrying about unexpected costs — highly suitable for beginners
* Leverage local mock environments (like LocalStack) when quick testing is needed to avoid unwanted billing risks

**Project Experience & Operations**
* Hackathon experiences help forge rapid problem-solving skills and teamwork under time pressure
* Project risks are often not what we visibly see (deadlines, bugs) but are hidden in deeper root causes: lack of proactive ownership and team cohesion
* Using AI in a project can create prolonged misunderstandings if not clearly defined and aligned from the beginning

**Psychology & Personal Development**
* Confidence can be "hacked" through specific, simple actions (counting down from 5 and doing it immediately) instead of waiting for the feeling of readiness
* Procrastination is largely not about laziness but hidden fears — identifying the true root cause is the first step to change

**Startup / Product**
* Even ideas in traditional fields (like horoscopes) can become tech products if properly combining AI with human expert verification
